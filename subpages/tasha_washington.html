Mt. Ellinor, Hoodsport (My First Mountain)

This was my first mountain!! Plus I hiked it solo! I read Mt. Ellinor was a good beginner mountain if you wanted to start climbing mountains. I knew I wasn't in the best of shape and had no idea what I was doing. I also knew, how else was I going to learn? Originally, my plan was to begin hiking at 3:00 AM so I could be at the top in time for sunrise. When I got there, however, it was so DARK, I waited until I could see the outline of the trees.


It took me longer to get up than it did to get down and it was exhausting! On the way down my legs were shaking with each step. I'm pretty sure there were murder hornets buzzing around me (I did report them), the sound of the bugs in the beginning sounded like I was listening to a soundtrack of a bee hive, and every time I thought I was almost to the top, I was humbly reminded I was not. I am so proud of myself for completing this hike, it was worth the challenge.

Glass Beach, Port Townsend

Glass Beach starts out as a sandy beach and quickly turns to rocky rubble the rest of the way. Out-and-back I believe it is a 5.5 mile trip. If you don't pay attention to the tide you can get stuck and if you're not prepared for the rocky treck it can be a long and tiring journey. This particular adventure I went alone because well, traveling with a toddler is not ideal. I made it to the end! Although I came looking for glass (and found some!) I came across far more interesting things. Like this starfish and the old driftwood tree stump.
